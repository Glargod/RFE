RFE

Robert Field Effect

A conceptual framework: apparent static equilibria are continuous, non-dissipative energy exchanges and configurational maintenance across scales.

Core Postulates

  • Continuum of energetic negotiation: No true rest state exists; every equilibrium is ongoing balance between cohesive and dispersive forces.
  • Constraint-induced deformation as activity: Compatible media (steel for magnets, bonds for atoms) enforce non-native geometry — persistent maintenance without macroscopic work.
  • Work redefined broadly: Classical F·dr is only one form; sustained balances, field coercion, Pauli enforcement, potential resistance all qualify as configurational "work".
  • Material-dependent activation: Effect activates with partners; incompatible ones (wood, vacuum) leave relaxed states where external potentials dominate.

Key Insight

Static stability is not the absence of process, but the most efficient, lossless mode of perpetual energetic interaction — a kind of "zero-dissipation work".

Potential energy is antijoules: energy invested to prevent relaxation. Equilibrium demands continuous (invisible) effort. There is no free energy — only pre-paid potential waiting behind barriers, extractable only at cost.

Origins & Scope

Born from magnets defying gravity on steel (vs. falling near wood), extended to atoms, nuclei, cosmic structures. Contradicts "equilibrium = nothing happening" to reveal hidden maintenance.

Scale matters: strongest where barriers are highest and decay slowest — from quantum zero-point to gravitational wells.

Antijoules Definitions

  • Antijoules (A): The capitalized (total invested) energetic cost a system has "pre-paid" and continues to pay invisibly to sustain its current configuration against all tendencies toward relaxation, dispersal, or collapse. Units: joules (or eV, GeV for atomic scales).
  • Barrier Energy (E_b): The energetic height/depth of the "well" or constraint preventing immediate relaxation (e.g., binding energy in atoms, coercivity in magnets, mgh in gravity).
  • Attempt Timescale (τ_attempt): The characteristic time between "challenges" to the equilibrium (e.g., vibrational periods in atoms ~10^{-15} s, spin-flip attempts in magnets ~10^{-9} s).
  • Lifetime Timescale (τ_lifetime): The expected duration the configuration holds before measurable leakage/decay.
  • Stability Factor (f_stability): A dimensionless term damping the rate-based cost (e.g., exp(-E_b / kT)).
  • Antijoule Power (P_A): The instantaneous rate of "expenditure" (watts) for maintenance — P_A = E_b / τ_attempt.
  • Levels: Hierarchical scales of negotiation (atomic, macro, gravity, magnetic).

Antijoules Formulas

Simplified Equation: For a single level/system

A ≈ E_b · (τ_lifetime / τ_attempt)

Antijoules scale with barrier strength times the number of "attempts" negotiated over the system's life.

Full Equation: For multi-scale systems

A_total ≈ ∑_levels [E_{b,level} · (τ_{lifetime,level} / τ_{attempt,level}) · f_{stability,level}] + ∑_{interactions} A_{cross}

Where f_stability = exp(-E_b / kT), and A_cross handles interactions between levels.

Applications Across Scales

  • Atomic Equilibrium: Nuclear binding A ≈ -Δm c²; electronic orbitals via ionization energies. Rate: P_A ≈ E_binding / τ_vibration.
  • Macro Atomic Bonds: Cohesive energy in solids A ≈ -∑ U_bonds. Rate via phonon timescales.
  • Gravity at Rest: A_grav ≈ mgh; ongoing stalemate against free-fall, layered on atomic supports.
  • Magnetic Conservation: A_magnetic ≈ -(exchange + anisotropy + domain energies). Rate: P_A ≈ E_coercive / τ_flip.
